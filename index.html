THE CONTEMPORARY WORLD

Global Migration

Political, Economic, Social, And Cultural Factors

Global migration refers to the movement of people across countries or regions to settle temporarily or permanently. It is a long-standing human phenomenon shaped by multiple interconnected factors. Migration is often explained through the “push and pull” model, where people are pushed away from their home countries and pulled toward better opportunities elsewhere.

01. Political Factors

• According to the Organization for International Migration there are approximately 192 million people live outside their place of birth

• A majority of these people are migrant workers and the make up 3% of the world's population

• Human being have always migrated from one place to another in search of better economic opportunities. but apart from economic factors, there are political factors that cause people to move from their home country to another country

State Persecution

• Includes harassment, discrimination, and torture

• Targets:

- Political opponents

- Religious minorities

- Ethnic Groups

• Forces people to migrate for safety

Lack of Political Liberties

• Lack of rights and freedom pushes people to leave

• Corruption and weak governance worsen conditions

• Often linked to poor economic situations

WARS AND CONFLICTS

• Around 42 million people are displaced due to war

• Causes large-scale forced migration

Key Term:

• Asylum Seeker: A person seeking protection due to fear of persecution

• Many asylum seekers face:

- Strict immigration policies

- Poor treatment in destination countries

• Some countries (e.g., in Europe) have strengthened border controls due to illegal migration concerns

02. Economic Factors

• Economic factors relate to the labor standards of a country, its unemployment situation and the overall health of its economy

• If economic conditions are poor or declining, more people tend to migrate to countries with better economies

• As low- and middle-income countries develop, migration to high-income countries may decrease.

MIGRANTS ARE ATTRACTED BY:

• HIGHER WAGES

• BETTER JOB OPPORTUNITIES
