Global Migration

Political, Economic, Social, And Cultural Factors

Global migration refers to the movement of people across countries or regions to settle temporarily or permanently. It is a long-standing human phenomenon shaped by multiple interconnected factors. Migration is often explained through the “push and pull” model, where people are pushed away from their home countries and pulled toward better opportunities elsewhere.

02. Economic Factors

• Economic factors relate to labor standards, unemployment, and the overall health of an economy.

• Poor or declining conditions drive migration toward healthier economies.

• Development in low-income countries may eventually decrease migration to high-income nations.

Migrants are attracted by:

• HIGHER WAGES

• BETTER JOB OPPORTUNITIES

• Escape from poor social and political conditions

03. Social Factors

Social factors encompass the personal and community influences that lead to migration. These include life milestones, infrastructure needs, and existing family networks.

Marital Status

• Women often migrate to their husband's home after marriage.

• Young unmarried women move seeking work opportunities.

Better Facilities

Seeking better services in:

  • Healthcare & Education
  • Transportation
  • Communication systems

Other Influences

• Family connections and relatives living abroad.

• Supportive social networks that facilitate migration.

• General desire for an improved quality of life.

04. Cultural Factors

"Some cultural groups seek their own identity and independence"

Migration is a transformation of the self. It involves the search for identity, the power of language, and the delicate balance between preserving heritage and blending into a new world.

CULTURE
Phase 01

Cultural Interaction

When cultures meet they may:

  • Learn from Each Other
  • Conflict Due to Differences
  • Dominate or Impose Beliefs on Others
Phase 02

Language and Power

  • Language Reflects Cultural Values
  • Used as a Tool for Communication and Influence
Phase 03

Adjustment and Belonging

Migrants often prefer places with:

  • Similar Language
  • Religion
  • Traditions

Moving to very different cultures can cause:

  • Culture Shock
  • Feeling Out of Place
Phase 04

Culture Adaptation

Many Immigrants:

  • Change Beliefs or Lifestyle
  • Adjust Behavior to Fit In

This Leads to:

  • Cultural Blending
  • Loss of Original Identity in Some Cases
